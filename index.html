Chinese Lucky Number 8

The number 8 is considered the luckiest number in Chinese culture due to its phonetic similarity to the word "fa" (发), which means to prosper, become wealthy, or achieve success. This association makes 8 a symbol of wealth, prosperity, and high social status, leading to its widespread preference in various aspects of life. For instance, the Beijing 2008 Olympic Games opening ceremony began at 8:08 PM on August 8, 2008, deliberately chosen to maximize the auspiciousness of the number.

The number 8 is highly favored in business, real estate, and personal decisions. Properties with the number 8 in their address or floor number often command 10–20% higher prices, and the 8th floor is typically the most expensive in buildings Phone numbers, car license plates, and gift amounts containing multiple 8s are considered especially lucky and can sell for significantly higher prices Combinations like 88 (representing double happiness) and 888 (triple prosperity) are particularly desirable

Beyond its general significance, 8 holds cultural and symbolic importance in Chinese mythology and philosophy. It is linked to the Eight Immortals in Taoist tradition and the Ba Gua (八卦), a set of eight trigrams used in cosmology and fengshui The number's symmetry, resembling the infinity symbol when rotated, further enhances its positive connotations While 4 is considered the most unlucky number due to its similarity to the word for death (死), 8 is actively sought after in both everyday and major life events
